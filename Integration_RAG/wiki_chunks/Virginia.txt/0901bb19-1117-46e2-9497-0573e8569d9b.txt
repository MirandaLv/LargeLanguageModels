White-tailed deer, one of 75 mammal species found in Virginia, rebounded from an estimated population of as few as 25,000 in the 1930s to over one million by the 2010s.[140][141] Native carnivorans include black bears, who have a population of around five to six thousand in the state,[142] as well as bobcats, coyotes, both gray and red foxes, raccoons, weasels and skunks.
