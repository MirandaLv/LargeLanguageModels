The expansion of federal government offices into Northern Virginia's suburbs during the Cold War boosted the region's population and economy.[84] The Central Intelligence Agency outgrew their offices in Foggy Bottom during the Korean War, and moved to Langley in 1961, in part due to a decision by the National Security Council that the agency relocate outside the District of Columbia.[85] The agency was involved in various Cold War events, and its headquarters was a target of Soviet espionage activities.
